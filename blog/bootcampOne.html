This is it! Bootcamp!

Foundations, done... Bootcamp ... lets go!!!!

So you've made it through foundations and bootcamp week has arrived. If you're reading this, and are wondering if a programming bootcamp is the right choice for you, you've probably come to the right place... lets dive on in over the next 9 weeks and find out what it's really like, from the perspective of someone who made the decision to go the bootcamp way.

Foundations was cool, pretty cruisy, but still a lot of work. I'd done a bit of HTML and CSS prior to foundations and many of the concepts I was already familiar with but there were a few new topics introduced.

I'd have liked more time to experiment with JavaScript coding, and if I was doing foundations again, I'd have got onto the more advanced JavaScript topics such as arrays and objects earlier on. But anyway, that's in the past, and we're at bootcamp.

In the days leading up to bootcamp I was pretty excited about it, and perhaps a little nervous. I'd heard it was very full on, and all you did was code, eat, and sleep, - with the majority proportion of that time assigned to code.

I'm quitting bootcamp!!!!

Day one arrived, and flew by as did most of the week. Introductions took up the first half day, and then we were into pair coding and JavaScript kata exercises. I really didn't enjoy the JavaScript Kata exercises in foundations. I found myself spending more time trying to understand what the question was actually asking you to do, than figuring out how to code it. Along with this, it seemed like I didn't know much JavaScript at all!

So you can imagine my 'annoyance' at more kata exercises! And then the same again on day two! Oh no, this isn't good. I'm over this! So I found myself walking home on Tuesday evening thinking about quitting. I figured, I can still teach myself everything, but in my own time, and at my own pace. Afterall, I've got the curriculum that outlines the topics we cover in the bootcamp, and You Tube is usually pretty good with tutorials these days.

So there I was... strolling home and wondering at what point you can pull out of the course and still get it classed as an academic withdrawal when it comes to student loans and NCEA regulations. I knew there's be a point at which this was possible, so I figured I'd check it out at some stage. I decided I'd be at course on Wednesday and see how it went.

Day 3 - We built a (small) project

The topic, and highlight of day three is .... No more Kata!!!! I heard on day 3 (or at least it was around day 3(the days become a blur)) that we weren't to be doing any more Kata exercises. This was brilliant news!!! We started working on a small project, just a simple task in reality, but we worked in pairs, with an overall view of the aim of the project and built it ourselves. A much better way to work! No more "guess what the question actually means" kind of stuff!!!!!

Day three was great, and the walk home so much more enjoyable! (oh, by the way, I use a lot of exclamation marks....!!) ! So the new plan was to hang around for week one, and that I should at least stay to the end of week two and then reacess how I was feeling about bootcamp.

Day 4 and 5, were also a blur. They were good days, and at this point I thought I really should write, or journal, this journey. And then I thought, why don't I attempt in my spare time (what spare time?) to update the blog we started in foundations. And so here I am, a few days later, at lunchtime sitting in the sun outside, with the laptop on my knees tappity tap tap tapping away at the keyboard writing a blog.

Overall impressions of week one

They say it's a journey. They say 'trust the process'. And I think they're right. Can I remember everything we covered in week one? No. Do I understand everything from week one? Far from it! But, the information is there, what I need to learn is there, and the experience is there.

At some point later in the week I recalled a phrase from Andy Sterkowitz, a You Tuber who talks around the programming scene. (Check out his YOU TUBE channel. Or check out a randomly selected video of his ...)

Anyway... as I was saying... In one of his You Tube videos, Andy had said, when recalling his decision to become a programmer, that it was something he wanted to do, and it didn't matter if it took him 3 months, 6 months, a year, 3 years or five years, - the time didn't matter. What mattered is that if he stuck at it, he WOULD in fact eventually get there.

My takeaway towards the end of week one was, that it didn't matter to me whether I 'pass' or 'fail' the course. And why should it? Why should I care? I'm here for the knowledge. I don't need to know it and understand it right now. Just take the knowledge, the information, and the journey, and store it away somewhere. Take notes, do the best you can for now, and in 10 weeks time (after bootcamp) you can study it, in your own time, at your pace, and fill in the gaps. And what's the hurry? I've got the remainder of the year to do this!

The people ...

You couldn't ask for a better team of people to spend the day with. The teachers are great. And the other students are great. The facilitators put a lot of energy into making the eda environment a friendly and awesome space. And the results show. There really isn't more to add in this paragraph. Words couldn't describe the positive, friendly, open, team oriented, environment that we're in, that has been created, and after only 5 days.

I enjoy learning from You Tube tutorials. I think it's a style that works for me - hearing things repeated often, it all becomes familiar. I found myself going to You Tube a few times this week, to a few of my favourite channels. Ania Kubow, does a pretty good job at explaining some concepts in short videos.

Kyle on Web Dev Simplified is another 'go to' channel. There's a few other channels I find useful which I might link to in future blogs. I started out bouncing around different channels when I started looking for tutorials, and I think that a list compiled with good channels could be useful. I've definitley now got my 'go to' channels..


And one last thing... I won't apologise for spelling mistakes, and bad syntax, or lack of creativity in my blogs for the next 9 weeks... they say bootcamp is full on... and they're right... there's no time to even tidy up blog posts or reword!!!! -So take it or leave it... stay safe, and enjoy the ride...
