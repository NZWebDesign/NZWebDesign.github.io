Emotional Intelligence

Understand Your Emotions, boost your emotional intelligence, achieve your goals, and build strong, lasting relationships.

What's Emotional Intelligence?

So you've made the leap into mindfulness, of living better, of being better, and you've heard about Emotional Intelligence. So what exactly is it? Emotional Intelligence is the ability to understand yourself. To understand yourself understanding yourself! To know yourself, to take time to reflect, to take time out, to understand and manage yourself. It is being in touch with your emotions - to perceive, evaluate and manage your emotions. By knowing and understanding yourself, you will know and understand others.

How's it different to IQ?

By contrast, IQ deals with Intelligence, the ability to apply logic, problem solve, to plan, learn new concepts and understand abstract ideas. In similar fashion to our ability to improve our IQ, we can also improve our EQ.

But why EI?

So why should I be so eager to get into this EI thing? Well, by understanding your emotions, and improving your emotional intelligence, research suggests you will be able to harness your emotions and feelings in positive ways to overcome challenges, reach those milestones, achieve goals in all aspects of life, communicate better, resolve conflict, and deal with stress - and who out there doesn't want those crucial advantages?!

So it's worth investing in your EI?

You heard it right here - it sure is worth investing time in building up your EI. Development of emotional intelligence can help you achieve and perform better at study, learning new things, at home, at work, and in social situations. It's often defined by the following four characteristics - Self Management, Self Awareness, and Relationship Management.

EI is desireable

Emotional Intelligence is ranked highly on the list of desirable attributes by employers seeking new employees. In a work environment a person with a high EI is often better equipped to deal with emotional and social situations that may arise at the work place. They fit in as both a learner and a leader. They work well in and with the team, and motivate the team as a whole to soar to new heights.

Take care

Another important aspect of EI is the ability to look after yourself. In an ever increasingly stressful world, the ability to understand yourself, to take time out, to reflect and recharge is highly sought after. All too often EI is neglected at the expense of hectic work hours, but before long the person is burnt out and is the next emotional breakdown. A strong EI can help a person manage and reduce stress, before it's too late. Stress has serious negative effects on a persons well being. This relates to both physical health and mental health. If nothing more, boost your EI for your own health and wellbeing.

Who do you know?

The old saying, "it's not what you know, it's who you know..." often rings true in the world, a world in which networking is vitally important. A high EI can help a person understand themselves socially, and help them to make positive connections with other people walking the same path. Rather than battling along on ones own, connections can be made through positive social networking as a result of being aware, and of having a positve understanding of EI.

So go forth, conquer the realm of Emotional Intelligence and you won't look back...
