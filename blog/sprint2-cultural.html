Learning Plan

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

The ultimate goal would be to be able to work remotely in website development or software engineering. Work that I can do while travelling would be ideal, which could be contract work or part-time. Initially, any jobs in the industry would be good, and for starting off it would probably be quite good to work with others in an office environment, or a few days a week in the office, with other more experienced people to bounce ideas off.

A description of your own strengths and limitations in relation to learning

My strengths include reliability, resiliance and creativeness. I think I have good organisational skills, and the ability to make plans, and see them through. However, I'm also open to changing the plans as progress is made. I'm hardworking and dependable, I enjoy working on my own but also work just as well as part of a team.

I often hear that inner voice telling me I can't do this, particularly if I see other people ahead of me, who seem to gave grasped a concept quicker. However, I also know that once I've understood the concept, then I can do just as well, and often better. I do like to keep things pretty relaxed and chill these days, perhaps some people might see this as not taking things serious enough.

What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

The ability to be confident as a web developer or programmer and I'd like to be more confident at job interviews. A recent phone interview for a developer job saw me really nervous, and I think this definitely came through in my phone manner.

What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you

It takes a lot for things to 'get to me.' However, on the odd occassion that something may have worked me up, I find the best thing to do is just go and take a break. It's easy enough to say in a friendly manner, "Oh wow, I need to get a break, back in a bit..." Grab a quick walk outside and then get back to it. And regardless - it's best to take regular breaks after an amount of work - 30 to 40 mins of work.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

I think I've got a good grasp on asking Google for help initially. After that, I find it easy enough to reach out to others on the course, and to tutors facilitators. I also think that EDA makes it quite easy to be able to do this, to reach out for help when you're stuck.
